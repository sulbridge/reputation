🔧 What is a boost?

Not a vague "action item." A concrete step.

Each boost is a specific, verifiable action that builds one of the signals AI engines actually use to decide who to recommend — executed at every one of your locations, not once for the brand as a whole. There are 28 distinct boost types below; a location typically needs 40–65 applied instances of them to reach AI‑recommended status (see why at the bottom).

Technical Foundation

5 boosts
  • Schema.org markup (Organization, LocalBusiness, FAQ, Article)

  • Mobile responsiveness audit
  • Robots.txt policy for AI crawlers (GPTBot, ClaudeBot, etc.)
  • JavaScript‑free content accessibility

Questions?

Frequently asked

Everything you need to know about AI visibility for multi‑location businesses.

What is AI visibility and why does it matter for multi‑location businesses?

AI visibility is how often and how positively a business is mentioned, cited, and recommended in AI-generated answers (ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Perplexity). For a chain or franchise, this happens location by location — an AI engine answering "best dentist near me" evaluates each individual clinic's NAP, reviews, and local content separately, not your brand as a whole.

Is this a monitoring tool, or do you actually do the work?

We do the work. ReputationGuardPro Enterprise runs the same boost‑by‑boost methodology as our single‑location plans — schema, NAP/directory consistency, local content, and review responses — executed by real experts at every one of your locations. Visibility monitoring and reporting exists too, but it's a byproduct of the execution, not the product itself; it's also available white‑label if you'd rather report it under your own brand.

How does pricing work across many locations?

You choose a tier based on how many expert actions each location needs per month. AI Visibility is the ongoing maintenance tier, billed annually per location since it runs indefinitely. AI Authority and AI Dominate are ramp‑up tiers, billed monthly for the 3–4 or 1.5–2 months it typically takes to reach AI‑recommended status — you're paying to compress the timeline, not committing to a year at that rate. Per‑location pricing drops as your total location count grows — see the calculator in the pricing section above. Minimum is 10 locations; below that, our single‑location plans at reputationguard.pro apply directly.

What happens once a location reaches AI‑recommended status?

If that location was on AI Authority or AI Dominate, it automatically steps down to AI Visibility‑tier maintenance pricing — this is mandatory, not optional. Once a location is recommended, the work left is upkeep: review responses, periodic content refresh, and monitoring — the same 5‑boosts‑a‑month workload as our maintenance tier, not the 15–30 boosts it took to get there. This means your blended bill across a portfolio naturally decreases over time as more locations graduate, and you're never paying ramp‑up pricing for a location that no longer needs ramping up.

How do you guarantee our data is secure?

All agents operate read‑only by default. Publishing, theme edits, and redirects require your explicit approval. Every boost, every location, is logged in your CRM with a timestamp and who approved it — exportable anytime for your own compliance review. We're GDPR/CCPA‑aligned today; SOC 2 is on our roadmap as we scale into larger enterprise engagements.

What kind of results can we expect and how fast?

Each location typically needs ≈40–65 applied boosts (out of 28 boost types — many, like NAP consistency or review responses, apply repeatedly) to reach AI‑recommended baseline visibility. How fast that happens depends on your tier: roughly 8–12 months per location on AI Visibility (5 boosts/mo), 3–4 months on AI Authority (15 boosts/mo), or 1.5–2 months on AI Dominate (30 boosts/mo). More locations means broader reach across your chain — it doesn't change the per‑location timeline, which is why we quote it per tier rather than as a blanket promise.

What happens if we acquire new locations mid‑contract?

New locations are onboarded at your current plan's per‑location rate and join the boost queue right away, starting with a baseline audit. There's no separate setup fee for locations added mid‑contract — they follow the same ramp‑up‑then‑maintenance path as the rest of your portfolio.

What if a location's score doesn't improve?

Your plan's visibility score improvement guarantee (60 days on AI Visibility, 30 on AI Authority, 21 on AI Dominate) means we keep working that location's boost backlog until the score moves. If a location genuinely doesn't respond within that window, we review the backlog for structural gaps — an incomplete Google Business Profile, unclaimed listings, and so on — and adjust the plan for that specific location.
